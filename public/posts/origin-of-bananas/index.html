The Surprising Origins of Bananas

Did you know that modern bananas are a product of thousands of years of selective breeding? The original wild bananas were full of seeds and nearly inedible. Today’s bananas are hybrids of two wild species: Musa acuminata and Musa balbisiana.

Bananas are technically berries, and their iconic “curve” happens because they grow against gravity—a process known as geotropism.

Fun Fact: Over 100 billion bananas are consumed globally each year, making them one of the most popular fruits on Earth!
